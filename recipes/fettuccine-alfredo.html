Fettuccine Alfredo

Description

Fettuccine Alfredo is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich, creamy sauce. The star of this dish is fettuccine, a type of flat, ribbon-like pasta that’s cooked until tender. The sauce is a luscious blend of butter, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese, which melts together to create a smooth and velvety coating for the pasta. A touch of garlic and a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper add depth and a hint of spice. Often garnished with a bit of chopped parsley, Fettuccine Alfredo is a decadent and comforting dish, perfect for those moments when you’re craving something indulgent yet simple.

Ingredients

  • Fettuccine Pasta
  • Butter
  • Cream
  • Salt, Pepper, and Garlic Salt
  • Romano Cheese
  • Parmesan Cheese

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add fettuccine and cook for 8 to 10 minutes; drain.
  2. Melt butter into cream in a large saucepan over low heat; add salt, pepper, and garlic salt. Increase the heat to medium; stir in grated Romano and Parmesan cheese until melted and sauce has thickened.
  3. Add cooked pasta to sauce and toss until thoroughly coated; serve immediately.
